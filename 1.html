Hebrew

Introduction

Hebrew is a Semitic language of the Afro-Asiatic language family spoken by more than seven million people in the world. It is the official language of Israel, and it is spoken by Jewish communities around the world.

Alphabet

Hebrew is written from right to left using an alphabet of 22 consonants. The alphabet is called "aleph-bet," after the first two letters of the Hebrew alphabet. The Hebrew alphabet is also used to write Yiddish and Ladino, which are Jewish languages.

In Hebrew, there are five letters that have different forms when they appear at the end of a word. These final forms are called “sofit” (סופית). Here they are:
  • ך (kaf sofit): This is the final form of the letter “kaf.”
  • ם (mem sofit): This is the final form of the letter “mem.”
  • ן (nun sofit): This is the final form of the letter “nun.”
  • ף (pe sofit): This is the final form of the letter “pe.”
  • ץ (tsadi sofit): This is the final form of the letter “tsadi.”
These letters are written differently when they appear at the end of a word, but their pronunciation remains the same. Interesting, isn’t it?

Grammar

Hebrew is a very old language, and its grammar is quite different from English. Hebrew has a system of roots and patterns that are used to create words. Hebrew verbs have different forms for different subjects and objects, and Hebrew nouns have different forms for different verb tenses.

First Phrases:

Here are some useful and basic Hebrew phrases:

  • Shalom - Hello = שלום
  • Toda - Thank you = תודה
  • Bevakasha - Please = בבקשה
  • Ken - Yes = כן
  • Lo - No = לא

1. The Hebrew Pronouns:

Hebrew pronouns are used to replace nouns in sentences. Here are the Hebrew pronouns:

  • Ani - I = אני
  • Atah - You (masculine) = אתה
  • At - You (feminine) = את
  • Hu - He = הוא
  • Hi - She = היא
  • Anarnu - We = אנחנו
  • Atem - You (plural masculine) = אתם
  • Aten - You (plural feminine) = אתן
  • Hen - They (plural feminine) = הן
  • Hem - They (plural masculine) = הם
